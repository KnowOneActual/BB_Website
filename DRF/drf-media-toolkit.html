Troubleshooting Library & Reference

Quick operational guidelines for resolving common boardroom hardware, EDID, HDCP, and networked audio errors.

EDID Handshake Failures
Symptoms: Display reports "No Signal", resolution is stretched or blurry.
Fixes: Force laptop output resolution manually to 1920x1080 @ 60Hz. On professional switchers (Crestron/Extron), set input EDID profiles to "Static" rather than "Dynamic". Power cycle the switch to force a fresh HDMI handshake.
HDCP Copyright Blocks
Symptoms: Laptop desktop is visible, but opening YouTube or training videos shows a completely black video block (audio plays).
Fixes: Disabling HDCP on professional switcher input ports forces the laptop browser to output standard unencrypted resolution instead of blocking the stream. Avoid daisy-chaining capture cards on the main display path.
Dante Networked Audio Dropouts
Symptoms: Audio completely drops out after exactly 2 to 5 minutes.
Root Cause: IGMP snooping is active on the network switch but **no IGMP Querier** is running. The switch terminates multicast streams when tables expire.
Fixes: Enable IGMP Querier on the AV VLAN switch settings. Disable Energy Efficient Ethernet (EEE / 802.3az) on all AV network ports to prevent clock synchronization jitters.
AV-over-IP Stream Stuttering
Symptoms: Video stream freezes, displays digital noise, or projector drops connection.
Fixes: Enable **Jumbo Frames** (MTU 9000) on the switches to prevent AVoIP packet fragmentation. Ensure switch interconnect links (uplinks) have at least 10 Gbps capacity or use Link Aggregation (LAG).

Operational Cautions

  • Audio sweeps and tones: High-frequency sweeps and reference tones can cause permanent hearing damage or damage speaker elements if played at excessive volumes. Always begin tests at minimum volume and increase level incrementally.
  • Network diagnostic scripts: Running automated ping sweeps or packet tests can trigger security alerts or saturate low-bandwidth links in corporate environments. Run only with appropriate administrative authorization.
  • Display convergence patterns: Do not leave static, high-contrast calibration grids on OLED or plasma displays for extended periods to avoid burn-in risks.
